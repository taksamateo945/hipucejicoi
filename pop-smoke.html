Early Life and Background of Pop Smoke

Pop Smoke, born on July 20, 1999, in Brooklyn, New York, had a unique upbringing that played a significant role in shaping his life and music career. He grew up in the neighborhood of Canarsie, where he faced various challenges and experiences that would later influence his music. His parents, Greg Jackson and Audrey Jackson, provided the foundation for his growth and development. Pop Smoke had an older brother named Obasi, who also played a role in his life.

Pop Smoke’s ethnicity is Jamaican-Panamanian, which reflects his cultural heritage and the diverse influences that would later shape his music. Throughout his childhood, he attended multiple schools, each offering a different perspective and exposure to different environments. These experiences would contribute to his unique sound and lyrical storytelling.

Despite the obstacles he faced, Pop Smoke was determined to make a name for himself in the music industry. However, his journey was not without its challenges. At the age of 15, he was expelled from eighth grade for bringing a gun to school. This incident was a turning point for him, as it led to his involvement in street life and ultimately shaped his music career.
